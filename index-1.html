UNDERSTANDING THE SUPPLY CHAIN

Super Stockist

In the supply chain is a critical link between manufacturers and distributors or retailers. This intermediary plays a pivotal role in optimising the distribution of products on a larger scale. Unlike regular distributors, super stockists are often responsible for handling bulk quantities of goods and serving a broader geographical area. They act as central hubs for warehousing, inventory management, and order consolidation. Super stockists streamline the supply chain by enabling manufacturers to consolidate their products in one location, reducing transportation costs and ensuring more efficient distribution to a network of distributors or retailers.

Distributor

In the supply chain refers to the pivotal role played by intermediaries, known as distributors, in facilitating the movement of goods from manufacturers to end customers. These distributors serve as a bridge between producers and retailers. Distributors leverage their market knowledge and established networks to efficiently distribute products to a wider customer base, allowing manufacturers to focus on production while benefiting from expanded market reach. Distributorship is a key component of supply chain optimization, ensuring that products are efficiently delivered to the right place at the right time.
